About the job

Overview

Microsoft is a company where passionate innovators come to collaborate, envision what can be and take their careers further. This is a world of more possibilities, more innovation, more openness, and the sky is the limit thinking in a cloud-enabled world.

Microsoft’s Azure Data engineering team is leading the transformation of analytics in the world of data with products like databases, data integration, big data analytics, messaging & real-time analytics, and business intelligence. The products our portfolio include Microsoft Fabric, Azure SQL DB, Azure Cosmos DB, Azure PostgreSQL, Azure Data Factory, Azure Synapse Analytics, Azure Service Bus, Azure Event Grid, and Power BI. Our mission is to build the data platform for the age of AI, powering a new class of data-first applications and driving a data culture.

Within Microsoft Fabric, the Platform team builds and operates the unified foundation that brings together all Fabric workloads into a single, cohesive experience. The platform provides shared infrastructure, governance, security, and a unified data model that enables customers to manage their entire data estate in one place. This team is central to delivering Fabric as a complete, end-to-end intelligence platform.

Within Fabric, Data Telemetry Infra builds telemetry pipelines for both internal and external customers. This is a mission critical service built for scale, compliance and data guarantees also leveraging our world class technologies like Power BI, Azure Data Explorer, EventHub, Azure Data Factory to ensure robust telemetry flow and ease of consumption for diagnostics and analytics

We do not just value differences or different perspectives. We seek them out and invite them in so we can tap into the collective power of everyone in the company. As a result, our customers are better served.

Responsibilities

  • Develop and maintain core-services, APIs and SDKs that are used to produce, pipeline and consume telemetry
  • Work with stakeholders to build and facilitate the usage of service data models, leveraging Azure and proprietary technologies
  • Drive improvements around areas of ownership in the service
  • Lead architecture, design, and develop features and solutions with high quality and scalability.
  • Design, implement, and refine chosen solutions in close partnership with Product Management and partner teams.
  • Be part of the on-call rotation for maintaining service health

Embody our culture and values

Every company has a mission. What's ours? To empower every person and every organization to achieve more. Microsoft operates in 190 countries and is made up of approximately 228,000 passionate employees worldwide.

Commitments

Environmental sustainability

Microsoft has public, time-bound commitments to become carbon negative, water positive, and zero waste in our operations by 2030. Microsoft is accelerating progress toward a more sustainable future by measuring and reducing our environmental footprint, accelerating research, helping our customers build sustainable solutions, and advocating for policies that benefit the environment.

Social impact

We have an enormous opportunity and responsibility to ensure that the technology we create benefits everyone on the planet, as well as the planet itself. As we drive economic growth, we must also commit to creating a more inclusive, equitable, sustainable, and trusted future for everyone. To help people and organizations everywhere achieve more, we focus on four commitments: support inclusive economic growth, protect fundamental rights, create a sustainable future, and earn trust.

Diversity, equity, and inclusion

At Microsoft, we strive to create an environment where people with different backgrounds, experiences, and perspectives thrive. Our journey and our learnings are ongoing, but we are clear about our goals:

We want every employee to feel like we can be our authentic selves, in alignment with our company values, so we can do our best work.

We’re focused on increasing representation, from interns all the way through to the boardroom. Our accountability starts at the highest levels of our organization and extends to every role.

We aim to influence systemic and sustainable change, which requires us to do the micro and macro motions every single day that impact employees’ experiences and transform the systems that drive access, opportunity, and greater equality.

We have a long history at Microsoft of applying our growth mindset to address some of the world’s most complex challenges. We put the same relentless focus and willingness to learn to work in confronting the realities of inequity in our industry and beyond—not because we have all the answers, but because we are uniquely positioned to make a difference when we innovate for inclusion.

Career growth and learning

At Microsoft, we believe a career is more than a job. It’s about who you are and who you want to become. It’s an ongoing experience—and with a growth mindset guiding the way, you can shape that experience into just about anything you can imagine.

We know that interests can shift and careers evolve over time. That’s why Microsoft is committed to helping our employees discover their passions, grow the skills they need to realize them, and connect with people who can support them throughout their career.

We support our employees’ development by focusing on three pillars of the career experience: discovery, connection, and growth. If they’re looking for inspiration, we provide resources that can help them discover new interests and engage with them in meaningful ways. We connect employees with the One Microsoft community and empower them to make the most of everything Microsoft’s global network has to offer. And we have an entire ecosystem of learning opportunities and experiences designed to help our employees grow professionally and personally.

Work-life balance

Microsoft is dedicated to providing comprehensive wellbeing benefits that help employees regenerate energy, at work and at home. While Microsoft as an organization is consistently taking a learn-it-all approach to better support its employees, a few examples of this dedication to employee wellbeing include:

A hybrid workplace model that allows employees to determine the working preferences that support how they work best. It’s understood and expected that employee preferences and needs may change over time—and that’s OK.

Comprehensive annual paid time off offerings and enhanced paid parental and family caregiver leave globally.

Access to mental health resources via our EAP, using our top-notch health plan, and on the Be Well platform—including digital therapy, self-directed sessions, prerecorded sessions with experts on resilience, physical activity contests, and more.
